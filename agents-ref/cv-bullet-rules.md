# Bullet Quality Rules

Shared standard for writing achievement bullets — applied by both `/personal-log` (when recording data) and `/draft-cv` (when writing CV bullets).

Bullets are the unit of credibility. Recruiters scan them to answer one question: *"What changed because of this person?"* If a bullet doesn't answer that, it doesn't belong.

---

## CAR Structure

Every bullet follows: **Context → Action → Result**

- **Context** — why the work mattered (the problem or situation)
- **Action** — specifically what *you* did (not what the team did)
- **Result** — "so what?" — without a result, the bullet describes a task, not an achievement

## Action Verbs

Open every bullet with a strong action verb. Verbs signal ownership: "Built" means you did it; "Responsible for" means you were assigned.

**Use:** Built, Designed, Delivered, Optimised, Diagnosed, Migrated, Architected, Led, Implemented, Refactored, Launched, Owned

**Avoid:** "Responsible for", "Helped with", "Assisted in", "Participated in", "Worked on"

## Measurable Results

At least 60% of bullets must include a measurable or specific result. Numbers are credibility anchors — they make claims verifiable and memorable.

- With numbers: *"~1,300 users with ~90% daily active rate within 1 month of launch"*
- Without numbers (still specific): *"consolidated payment logic into the new system while ensuring zero disruption to existing flows"*
- Too vague (avoid): *"improved performance"*, *"enhanced the user experience"*

## Length and Format

- Keep each bullet to 1–2 lines. If running long, split into two.
- Spell out acronyms on first use: "Server-Side Rendering (SSR)", "Customer Relationship Management (CRM)"

---

## Weak vs Strong Examples

**Weak:** "Helped with performance optimization for the dashboard."

**Strong:** "Rebuilt the analytics dashboard — migrated legacy class components to React hooks and introduced a shared component library, reducing average page load time from 4.2s to 1.1s."

---

**Weak:** "Responsible for migrating the payment system to the new architecture."

**Strong:** "Migrated the legacy payment system — investigated handler logic, mapped API endpoint structures, and consolidated flows into the new system, ensuring zero disruption to existing transactions."
